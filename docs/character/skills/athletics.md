# Athletics

Athletics covers difficult situations you encounter while climbing, jumping, or swimming. It also covers feats of raw physical power.

## How to Roll

**Athletics = d20 + Strength modifier + Proficiency (if proficient)**

## Common Uses

### Climb
Scale vertical surfaces. The DC depends on the surface:
- **DC 10**: A surface with plenty of handholds (rope, ladder, rocky cliff)
- **DC 15**: A rough wall or tree with some handholds
- **DC 20**: A smooth wall with minimal handholds
- **DC 25+**: A nearly sheer surface or overhang

### Jump
Leap across gaps or over obstacles. Long jumps cover a number of feet equal to your Strength score (with a 10-foot running start). High jumps reach 3 + Strength modifier feet. Athletics checks extend these distances.

### Swim
Move through water, especially rough or turbulent water.
- **DC 10**: Calm water
- **DC 15**: Choppy water or a mild current
- **DC 20**: Rough water or a strong current
- **DC 25+**: Stormy seas or rapids

### Grapple
Grab and restrain a creature. Contest your Athletics against their Athletics or Acrobatics.

### Shove
Push a creature away or knock them prone. Contest your Athletics against their Athletics or Acrobatics.

## Proficiency Benefits
- **Charge**: You can move up to your speed in a straight line and make a melee attack with advantage.
- **Sprint**: You can double your movement speed for one round.

## Expertise Benefits
- **Leaping Attack**: You can make attacks while jumping without penalty.

## Mastery Benefits
- You automatically succeed on Athletics checks of DC 15 or lower.
- Your carrying capacity is doubled.
