# Persuasion

Persuasion is used when you attempt to influence someone or a group of people with tact, social graces, or good nature. You use Persuasion when acting in good faith.

## How to Roll

**Persuasion = d20 + Charisma modifier + Proficiency (if proficient)**

## Common Uses

### Make a Request
Convince someone to do something for you. The DC depends on what you're asking:
- **DC 10**: A small favor that costs them little
- **DC 15**: A moderate request or something slightly inconvenient
- **DC 20**: A significant favor or something they'd rather not do
- **DC 25+**: A major sacrifice or something against their interests

### Negotiate
Reach a mutually beneficial agreement, haggle over prices, or broker a deal.

### Change Attitude
Improve how someone feels about you or an idea. This typically shifts their attitude one step (hostile → unfriendly → indifferent → friendly → helpful).

### Gather Information
Talk to locals to learn rumors, news, or information about a topic.

### Make a Good Impression
Charm someone in a social situation, leaving them with a positive view of you.

## Proficiency Benefits
- You can shift attitudes by two steps with an exceptional roll.
- You gain advantage on Persuasion with creatures who are already friendly to you.

## Expertise Benefits
- You can negotiate effectively even with hostile creatures.
- Your successful persuasion attempts create lasting positive impressions.

## Mastery Benefits
- You automatically succeed on simple requests from indifferent or friendly creatures.
- You can sense what arguments would be most persuasive to a given individual.
