# Investigation

Investigation is used when you look around for clues and make deductions based on those clues. It covers searching for hidden objects, deducing the location of a hidden object, or piecing together clues to solve a mystery.

## How to Roll

**Investigation = d20 + Intelligence modifier + Proficiency (if proficient)**

## Common Uses

### Search for Clues
Examine an area or object for hidden details, secret compartments, or evidence of tampering.
- **DC 10**: Obvious clues that are merely overlooked
- **DC 15**: Hidden clues that require careful examination
- **DC 20**: Well-concealed clues or subtle evidence
- **DC 25+**: Expertly hidden evidence or magical concealment

### Deduce Information
Draw conclusions from available evidence. Figure out what happened, who was involved, or where something might be hidden.

### Find Hidden Objects
Locate secret doors, hidden compartments, or concealed items through logical deduction about where they might be.

### Solve Puzzles
Work out the solution to riddles, puzzles, or coded messages.

### Research
Find specific information in books, documents, or other written sources.

## Proficiency Benefits
- You can search an area in half the normal time.
- You notice when something is "off" about a scene, even before searching.

## Expertise Benefits
- You can deduce information about a creature or object with minimal evidence.
- You gain advantage on checks to find hidden doors or traps.

## Mastery Benefits
- You automatically notice obvious clues without searching.
- You can reconstruct events from minimal evidence (similar to forensic analysis).
