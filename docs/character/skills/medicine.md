# Medicine

Medicine lets you try to stabilize a dying companion or diagnose an illness.

## How to Roll

**Medicine = d20 + Wisdom modifier + Proficiency (if proficient)**

## Common Uses

### Stabilize a Dying Creature
Stop a creature at 0 hit points from dying. DC 10.

### Diagnose Illness
Identify what disease or condition is affecting a creature.
- **DC 10**: Common ailments (cold, flu, food poisoning)
- **DC 15**: Uncommon diseases or conditions
- **DC 20**: Rare diseases or magical ailments
- **DC 25+**: Unknown or magically obscured conditions

### Treat Wounds
Provide first aid to reduce bleeding, prevent infection, or otherwise care for injuries.

### Determine Cause of Death
Examine a corpse to determine how and when they died.

### Perform Surgery
Requires proficiency. Remove foreign objects, set bones, or perform other surgical procedures.

### Identify Poison
Determine what poison is affecting a creature and potentially how to treat it.

## Proficiency Benefits
- You can perform surgery and other procedures requiring training.
- Creatures you stabilize regain 1 hit point.

## Expertise Benefits
- You can treat wounds during a short rest, granting bonus hit dice recovery.
- You can identify poisons and diseases automatically if you've encountered them before.

## Mastery Benefits
- You can stabilize creatures as a bonus action.
- Your long-term care doubles a creature's natural healing rate.
