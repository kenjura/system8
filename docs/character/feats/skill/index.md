Skill feats are traits available to all PCs that reflect the expertise and training they have acquired in various skills. 


# Strength Skill Feats

- [Athletic](#): Your Dash distance increases by your Strength bonus. You gain a Jump, Swim, and Climb speed equal to your Strength bonus in squares. You gain a Sprint action (at-will, std; move up to four times your base movement speed in a straight line; you can end this movement early; you cannot make attacks or use powers during this movement; you stop if you hit an obstacle).



# Dexterity Skill Feats

- [Acrobatic](#): You gain a Tumble action (at-will, move; move up to half your base movement speed while avoiding opportunity attacks; you can end this movement early; you cannot make attacks or use powers during this movement). You gain the Evasion ability (at-will, reaction to area effect; you jump up to 1 square per Dex bonus to a safe square to avoid the effect). You ignore 10 feet of falling damage per point of your Dexterity bonus.
- [Sleight of Hand](#): You can draw or stow a weapon or item as a free action. You can pick pockets, plant items, or perform legerdemain without being noticed if your target is distracted or unaware of you. You can disable simple traps and locks without tools, and you gain advantage when using thieves' tools.
- [Stealthy](#): You can attempt to Hide even when being observed, as long as you have some form of cover or concealment. You can move at full speed while hidden without penalty to remaining hidden.



# Intelligence Skill Feats

- [Arcane Lore](#): You can identify spells being cast as a free action. You can attempt to recall useful information about magical creatures, items, or phenomena. You gain advantage on checks to decipher magical writing, identify potions, or recognize spell effects.
- [Historical Lore](#): You can recall significant events, legendary figures, ancient kingdoms, and past disputes. When encountering ruins, artifacts, or heraldry, you can attempt to identify their origin and significance. You gain advantage on checks to appraise antiques and historical artifacts.
- [Investigative](#): You can search a room or area thoroughly in half the normal time. When examining a scene, you notice hidden compartments, secret doors, and subtle clues that others miss. You can deduce how events unfolded based on physical evidence.
- [Natural Lore](#): You can identify plants, animals, terrain types, and weather patterns. You know which creatures are natural and which are magical or extraplanar. You can predict natural phenomena like storms, earthquakes, or animal migrations.
- [Religious Lore](#): You can identify holy symbols, religious rites, and divine creatures. You know the tenets and histories of major religions and cults. You can recognize undead types and recall their weaknesses, and you gain advantage on checks to recall information about fiends, celestials, and undead.



# Wisdom Skill Feats

- [Animal Handler](#): You can calm a hostile beast or gain a mount's trust with a few moments of interaction. You can train animals in half the normal time. While mounted, you gain advantage on checks to control your mount, and your mount gains +2 to AC against attacks targeting it.
- [Insightful](#): You can sense when someone is lying or hiding something, gaining advantage on checks to detect deception. You can read a person's general emotional state and intentions after a brief conversation. Once per encounter, you can ask the GM one yes/no question about an NPC's motivations.
- [Medical Training](#): You can stabilize a dying creature as a swift action instead of a standard action. You can diagnose diseases and poisons, and you know their typical treatments. When you use a healing kit or administer a potion, the target regains additional HP equal to your Wisdom bonus.
- [Perceptive](#): You cannot be surprised while conscious. You gain advantage on checks to notice hidden creatures, traps, or secret doors. You can lip-read conversations you can see but not hear.
- [Survival Training](#): You can track creatures through any terrain, and you can identify tracks to determine creature type, number, and how recently they passed. You can find food and water for yourself and up to five others in any environment. You gain advantage on checks to navigate and avoid natural hazards.



# Charisma Skill Feats

- [Deceptive](#): You can lie convincingly under pressure, even when your story has obvious holes. You can maintain a false identity indefinitely, and you gain advantage on checks to disguise yourself or forge documents. You can feint in combat as a swift action, causing your next attack against that target to have advantage.
- [Intimidating](#): You can demoralize an enemy as a swift action (at-will; target within 6 squares who can see or hear you becomes frightened of you until the end of your next turn; Will negates). Creatures you have demoralized take a penalty equal to your proficiency bonus on their next attack roll against you.
- [Performer](#): You can captivate an audience, earning income during downtime or gaining favorable reactions from crowds. You can use performance to create distractions, allowing allies to hide or move unnoticed. You gain advantage on checks to disguise your voice or impersonate someone's mannerisms.
- [Persuasive](#): You can negotiate favorable terms in deals and disputes. You can request aid, information, or shelter from friendly or neutral NPCs with greater success. Once per day, you can turn a hostile NPC indifferent (or an indifferent NPC friendly) if you can speak with them for at least one minute.
